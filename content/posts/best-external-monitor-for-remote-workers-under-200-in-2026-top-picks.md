## Additional Buying Tips for Remote Workers

When shopping for the best external monitor for remote workers under $200, it's important to consider a few key factors that can significantly impact your work experience. First, think about the size of the screen. A larger screen can help reduce eye strain and make multitasking easier, but it also requires more desk space. A 24-inch monitor is a popular choice as it offers a good balance between size and price. Additionally, consider the resolution. While a Full HD (1920x1080) resolution is standard and sufficient for most tasks, a higher resolution can provide a clearer and more detailed display, which is especially beneficial for graphic designers or video editors.

Another important aspect to look at is the connectivity options. Ensure that the monitor you choose has the necessary ports to connect to your computer, such as HDMI, DisplayPort, or USB-C. This can vary depending on your computer's specifications, so it's crucial to check compatibility beforehand. Lastly, don't overlook the importance of a good stand. A monitor with a height-adjustable stand can help you maintain proper posture, reducing the risk of neck and back pain during long hours of work.
